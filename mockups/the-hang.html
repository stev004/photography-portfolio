Exhibition walkthroughs built from the archive: which frames earn wall space, what they're printed on, and exactly how they hang — wall by wall, to scale. Edition 01 is the flagship two-room show; Edition 02 is the all-spider follow-up; and The Season, at the bottom, maps how many more shows the current portfolio can carry.

Edition 01 — “Field Work”

Flagship · two rooms · 16 works

The archive splits cleanly into two bodies that flatter each other: digital macro — sharp, intimate, scientifically literate — and the 35mm diary — loose, warm, grain-forward. Fighting that split would water both down; the show leans into it instead. Room A, “Undergrowth”, is painted charcoal and lit with tight spots so the frameless prints float like specimens in a vitrine. Room B, “Roads South”, is standard gallery white with oak-framed C-types at postcard-modest sizes. You walk from the world at 2:1 magnification into the world at 1:50,000 — same photographer, same patience, different lens.

The run-through

If you actually book the room

01 · PRINTING

Match medium to source

  • Digital macro (R7, 32 MP): dibond or acrylic, full bleed, matte laminate. Safe to 100 × 150; the 40-frame stack will hold detail at any size you can afford.
  • 35mm scans: cap at 50 × 75. Grain is the aesthetic, mush isn't. C-type on a matte stock, never gloss.
  • Order one proof print of the cobra and one film frame before committing the run — check blacks on dibond and the red channel on Portra scans.
02 · FRAMING

Two specs, no exceptions

  • Room A: frameless dibond with hidden subframe — the image floats 2 cm off the wall.
  • Room B: 25 mm natural oak, white 7 cm mat, art glass (anti-reflective) on everything under spots.
  • Uniform specs are what make 16 works read as one show — resist a special frame for a favourite.
03 · HANGING

152 cm and a laser level

  • Centre of every image at 152 cm. Groups share the centreline, not top edges.
  • Gaps: 24 cm inside a group, 1.5–2 m around heroes (walls 1 and 6).
  • Hang order: hero first, then walls 2–3, then the diary line — if space fights back, the pairs flex, the heroes don't.
04 · LIGHTING

Room A dark, Room B bright

  • Room A: walls in charcoal (F&B Off-Black or equivalent), tight 3000 K spots at 30°, one per work. Ambient low.
  • Room B: even wash, same 3000 K — don't mix colour temperatures mid-show.
  • Dibond hates glare: aim spots so reflections fall below eye line.
05 · LABELS & EDITIONS

The science is the label

  • Labels: title, species in italics, capture data (lens, aperture, stack count), stock for film works, price. Your BRAND.md voice, verbatim.
  • One QR per room to the portfolio site, not one per work.
  • Editions as per hang list; sign and number verso, certificate for the hero.
06 · MONEY & OPENING

Rough budget

  • Printing + framing all 16: expect £1,800–2,400 at UK lab rates; the oak frames are half of it.
  • Full sell-through at guide prices ≈ £3,940 on first editions alone — the show pays for itself at ~60% sold.
  • Opening night: species-ID cards as flyers, the Print Room mockups on a screen, postcards (chapel + palms) at the till for £3 — the low-commitment purchase every visitor makes.

Edition 02 — “Eight Eyes”

All-spider show · one dark room · 9 works

The spider work is the deepest seam in the archive — twelve strong frames and counting — and it deserves a show that doesn't share billing. One room, near-black walls, tight pools of light: the gallery becomes the underside of a leaf. Where Edition 01 impresses, Edition 02 converts — people arrive wary of spiders and leave having stood nose-to-nose with one for ten minutes.

01 · THE AMBUSH · 120 × 80 · DIBOND

Four gold-rimmed eyes emerging from darkness — on a near-black wall the print has no visible edge, so the eyes simply hang in the room. The single strongest opener in the whole archive; it sets the show's rule that the dark is full of watchers.

The Season — how many shows are in the archive?

Census of ~46 frames · grouped by theme · readiness rated

Full audit of the portfolio: two shows are hangable today, one is a print-order away, and three more are each one good shoot from opening. That's a two-year exhibition programme already sitting on your hard drive. Readiness counts only frames strong enough to print large.

SHOW 1 · READY — MOCKED ABOVE

Field Work

16 works · flagship. The two-room macro + film survey, fully specced above. The one to pitch to a proper gallery first — it shows range.

SHOW 2 · READY — MOCKED ABOVE

Eight Eyes

9–12 works · one dark room. The all-spider show. Deepest bench in the archive (The Ambush, the Watcher portraits, the behaviour set). Perfect for a science-festival or museum slot, where the species labels become the draw.

SHOW 3 · A PRINT ORDER AWAY

Roads South

~14 works · all 35mm. Dusk, Villa, Chapel, Palms, Blue Plains, Half Turn (the ferris wheel), Reclaimed (the overgrown Coke machine), Hibiscus, Garda, Boats… A warm film-diary show — café-gallery or photography-festival fringe, framed small and priced to sell.

SHOW 4 · ONE ZOO VISIT AWAY

Vivarium

6 works now, needs ~10. Cobra, Mangshan viper, chameleon, crocodile lizard, croc skink, sloth. The reptile-house portraits are already museum-grade — one more session of glass-front portraits finishes a genuinely unusual show.

SHOW 5 · ONE SPRING AWAY

Hortus

7 works now, needs ~12. Buttercup, viola, both dianthus, white geranium, alkanet, the butterfly. The lit-flower-on-black formula is repeatable on demand — one spring of shooting completes a set that sells as prints better than anything else here.

SHOW 6 · POP-UP READY

Paddock

7 works. Ferrari badge ×2, Sauber, both Miuras, Escort at night, Fabia service park. Not a white-cube show — a pop-up at a motor event or club night, hung on scaffold, sold as A2 posters. Different audience, fastest money.

Draft statement

Wall vinyl · Room A entrance · your voice, not art-speak

Most of these photographs were taken within a few miles of home. The spiders live in the hedge; the snakes and chameleons live behind glass; the roads are the ones we drove. Everything is exactly as found — the only trick is patience, and getting low enough to meet things at their own eye level.

— STEVEN MATSON · JERSEY, CHANNEL ISLANDS · FIELD WORK, 2026
