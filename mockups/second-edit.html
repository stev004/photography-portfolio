The verdict

One sentence: the portfolio is using a tenth of what you have

The current portfolio carries 22 film frames. This desktop alone holds 39 more A-grade frames — including an entire black & white body of work nobody has seen, a Villa d'Este roll dense enough to double the car portfolio on its own, and the missing chapters of Roads South. A taste check worth noting: grading blind, I picked three frames that turned out to already be in the portfolio (Dusk, Sunlit Chapel, the Escort). We agree on what your best work looks like.

What stayed behind — and what's missing

The honest half of the edit

  • The disposable party rolls (9657/9658) are life, not portfolio. Friends, parties, mirrors, the poker table. Two exceptions made the cut (the plane-window pair). Everything with a recognisable face I left out on principle — publishing needs consent, and their value is personal anyway.
  • ~180 frames are honest B/C material: duplicate compositions (you often shoot 2–3 of the same scene — good habit, only one survives the edit), underexposed night interiors, and mid-distance landscapes without a subject.
  • Gap: black & white. One roll of HP5 in the whole archive, and it produced five A-grades. That ratio is a signal — shoot more of it.
  • Gap: people. The two HP5 portraits are the only deliberate portraits in 371 frames, and they're excellent. A permission-based portrait habit would open a seventh show.
  • Gap: weather. Almost every frame is fair-weather. The archive has no rain, no fog, no winter sea — Jersey gives those away free.
  • Still unseen: the three Cinestill rolls and the Fujifilm roll in "film pics 2" (~255 frames), plus the entire digital macro catalogue — both on the laptop. Census two happens whenever that machine and I are in the same room.
